Hydraulic Operations & Cost Estimation

JIC symbols, hydraulic valves, machine circuits, and production cost estimation in foundry, welding & machine shops. (Prof. Jinu — JNM)

💧 Introduction to Hydraulic Systems

Hydraulic systems use pressurized oil (hydraulic fluid) to transmit power and perform work. In machine tools, hydraulic systems provide smooth, stepless speed variation and high forces at low speeds.

Basic Elements of a Hydraulic Circuit

  • Hydraulic pump — converts mechanical energy to hydraulic energy (pressure + flow)
  • Actuators — cylinders (linear) or motors (rotary) that convert hydraulic energy to mechanical work
  • Control valves — pressure, flow, and direction control valves
  • Reservoir (tank) — stores hydraulic oil
  • Filters — clean the oil
  • Relief valve — limits maximum system pressure (safety)
  • Piping and fittings — connect components

📐 JIC Symbols

JIC (Joint Industry Council) symbols are standardized symbolic drawings used in schematics to represent hydraulic components.

Valve Notation System

Valve function given as P/Q where:

  • P = number of main ports (inlets, outlets, exhausts)
  • Q = number of states/positions the valve can achieve
ValveDescription
2/2 DCV2 ports, 2 positions — simple on/off valve
3/2 DCV3 ports (inlet, outlet, exhaust), 2 positions — directional control
4/2 DCV4 ports, 2 positions — reverses cylinder direction
4/3 DCV4 ports, 3 positions — most common; center position options: all-blocked, semi-open, open-center

🔧 Hydraulic Valves

1. Pressure Control Valves

  • Relief Valve: Protects pump and lines from excessive pressure. When pressure exceeds setting, valve opens and returns fluid to reservoir. Sets maximum system pressure.
  • Pressure Reducing Valve: Sets maximum pressure at the valve outlet — controlled output pressure lower than inlet pressure. Used in branch circuits requiring lower pressure.

2. Flow Control Valves

  • Restrictor (Throttle valve): Controls oil flow rate, thus controlling actuator speed. Has adjustable orifice.
  • Restrictor with Check Valve: Allows free flow in one direction, restricted flow in the other.

3. Directional Control Valves (DCV)

  • Controls direction of fluid flow to actuators.
  • Operated by: lever, push button, solenoid, pilot pressure, spring, roller.
  • 4/3 DCV most common — controls double-acting cylinder (extend, retract, hold).

Other Components

  • Check valve: Allows oil in one direction only.
  • Accumulator: Pressure storage reservoir — enables system to cope with extreme demand, smooths out pulsations.
  • Cylinder cushioning: Slows cylinder just before it reaches end of stroke to prevent shock.

🔌 Hydraulic Circuit Elements

Basic Hydraulic Power Pack

Electric Motor → Pump → Relief Valve → Filter → Tank → Actuator circuit.

Types of Hydraulic Circuits

  • Open center circuit: Allows flow on both sides when valve is in center position.
  • Closed center (all-blocked): No flow in system when valve centered — pump pressure builds.
  • Tandem center: Allows flow to tank when closed; pump unloaded.

⚙️ Hydraulic Circuit: Shaper Machine

Basic Elements

  • Constant discharge hydraulic pump
  • Pressure relief valve
  • 4-way (4/3) direction control valve (DCV)
  • Double-acting hydraulic cylinder with piston rod

Working

  • Hydraulic pump (constant discharge) delivers oil to the 4/3 direction control valve.
  • If pressure exceeds set limit, relief valve opens and returns oil to reservoir.
  • Forward (cutting) stroke: Valve directs oil to right side (full bore end) of cylinder through port A → piston moves left → tool cuts workpiece. Oil from left side returns to tank through port B and P.
  • Return stroke: Valve shifts → oil enters left side (rod end) through B → piston moves right. Oil from right side returns through A and P.
  • Quick return: Rod side has smaller area → same oil flow results in higher piston speed during return stroke (automatically faster).

🔩 Hydraulic Circuit: Drilling Machine

In a hydraulic drilling machine, the drill spindle is driven by a hydraulic motor and the feed is provided by a hydraulic cylinder.

Components

  • Hydraulic pump (variable displacement)
  • Pressure relief valve (system safety)
  • 4/3 DCV — controls drill spindle direction and feed cylinder
  • Flow control valve (throttle) — controls feed rate
  • Check valve — prevents reverse flow
  • Hydraulic motor — drives drill spindle
  • Feed cylinder — controls axial feed of drill

Working

  • Motor drives the drill spindle at speed set by flow control valve.
  • Feed cylinder advances the drill at controlled feed rate.
  • At end of stroke, DCV shifts to retract the drill (return stroke).
  • Flow control valve on feed cylinder ensures constant feed rate regardless of drilling resistance.

💰 Production Cost Estimation

Production cost estimation is the process of calculating the total cost of manufacturing a component before it is actually produced.

Elements of Total Manufacturing Cost

  1. Material Cost — direct material cost (raw material + process scrap adjustment)
  2. Labour Cost — direct labour for manufacturing operations
  3. Direct Other Expenses — patterns, core boxes, power consumption, fixtures
  4. Overhead Expenses — supervisory staff, administration, lighting, water, depreciation (expressed as % of labour cost)
Total Cost = Material Cost + Labour Cost + Direct Expenses + Overheads Factory Cost = Material Cost + Labour Cost + Direct Expenses + Factory Overheads Cost of Production = Factory Cost + Administrative/Office Overheads Selling Price = Cost of Production + Sales Overhead + Profit

🏭 Cost Estimation in Foundry Shop

A foundry shop consists of: Pattern Making → Sand Mixing → Core Making → Mould Making → Melting → Pouring → Fettling → Inspection.

Material Cost of Casting

Step 1: Net weight = Volume of casting × Density Step 2: Add process scrap weight (runners, gates, risers) Step 3: Add allowance for metal loss (oxidation, over-runs) Step 4: Total weight × Cost per unit weight = Gross material cost Step 5: Subtract scrap return value Direct Material Cost = Gross material cost − Scrap return value

Labour Cost

  • Core making, mould making: based on time taken.
  • Melting, pouring, fettling, cleaning: calculated on per kg of cast weight basis.

Overheads

Expressed as percentage of labour charges or per kg of casting weight. Includes: supervisory staff, pattern shop, inspection, admin, electricity, water.

🔥 Cost Estimation in Welding Shop

Cost Elements in Welding

  1. Direct Material Cost:
    • Cost of base materials (sheet, plate, section)
    • Cost of electrodes/filler material
    Weight of weld metal = Volume of weld × Density of weld material
  2. Direct Labour Cost:
    • Preparation (edge preparation, machining)
    • Actual welding (arc-on time)
    • Finishing (grinding, heat treatment, painting)
  3. Direct Other Expenses:
    • Power consumed in arc welding:
    Power Cost = Voltage × Current × Arc-on time × Power cost per unit
    • Cost of fixtures used
  4. Overheads: Office/supervisory staff, lighting, depreciation, consumables.

⚙️ Cost Estimation in Machine Shop

Cost Elements

  1. Material Cost — raw material (bar stock, forging, etc.)
  2. Machining Cost — based on time per operation × rate per hour
  3. Tool Cost — amortized tool life cost per piece
  4. Setup Cost — machine setup time × rate
  5. Overheads — machine depreciation, power, operator overhead

Machining Time Formula

For Lathe (turning): Machining time = L / (f × N) where L = length of cut (mm), f = feed (mm/rev), N = speed (rpm) For Drilling: Machining time = (L + approach) / (f × N) approach = D / (2 × tan(θ)) where θ = drill point half-angle Machining cost = Time × Rate per minute

Sheet Metal Shop Costing

  • Material cost: area of blank × thickness × density × cost per kg
  • Plus scrap allowance, die amortization, press time × rate
  • Sheet metal operations: blanking, punching, bending, drawing

✏️ Solved Problems

Problem 1 — Foundry Cost: Calculate total cost of CI casting

Given:

  • Component: CI cap, density = 7.2 g/cc
  • Cost of molten iron = Rs. 30 per kg
  • Process scrap = 17% of net weight
  • Process scrap return value = Rs. 5 per kg
  • Administrative overhead = Rs. 2 per kg of metal poured

(Assume net volume of casting = 200 cc from drawing)

1
Net weight of casting:
Net weight = Volume × Density = 200 cc × 7.2 g/cc = 1440 g = 1.44 kg
2
Process scrap weight:
Scrap = 17% of 1.44 = 0.245 kg
Total weight (gross) = 1.44 + 0.245 = 1.685 kg
3
Material cost:
Gross material cost = 1.685 × Rs. 30 = Rs. 50.55
Scrap return = 0.245 × Rs. 5 = Rs. 1.225
Net material cost = 50.55 − 1.225 = Rs. 49.33
4
Overhead charges:
Admin overhead = 1.685 × Rs. 2 = Rs. 3.37
5
Total cost = Rs. 49.33 + Rs. 3.37 = Rs. 52.70 per casting

Given: 150 components (50 mm dia × 20 mm length head + 20 mm dia × 100 mm length shank), made by upsetting a 20 mm diameter bar. Density = 7.86 g/cc.

1
Net volume of one component:
Volume of head: π/4 × 50² × 20 = 39,270 mm³
Volume of shank: π/4 × 20² × 100 = 31,416 mm³
Net volume = 39,270 + 31,416 = 70,686 mm³ = 70.686 cc
2

Net weight = 70.686 × 7.86 g/cc = 555.6 g = 0.556 kg
3
Total net weight for 150 components:
150 × 0.556 = 83.4 kg
4
Length of 20mm bar required per component:
Volume of bar needed = Net volume (conservation of volume in forging)
= 70,686 mm³
Length = 70,686 / (π/4 × 20²) = 70,686 / 314.16 = 225 mm per component
5
Gross weight of 20mm bar:
Volume per bar = 70,686 mm³
Gross weight per component = 70,686 mm³ × 7.86×10⁻³ g/mm³ = 555.6 g

JIC Symbols for Flow Control Valves:

  • Fixed restrictor: Rectangle with diagonal line — creates fixed flow restriction; used where flow rate is preset
  • Adjustable FCV: Rectangle with diagonal line plus arrow through it — arrow indicates variable setting; used for variable cylinder speed control
  • Check valve: Triangle pointing in flow direction with a bar — allows free flow one way only; blocks reverse flow
  • FCV with check valve: The adjustable restrictor in parallel with a check valve — restricts flow in one direction (metering), free flow in reverse (rapid return)

Application: FCV is used in drilling, grinding, and shaper machine circuits to control the cylinder feed rate by restricting oil flow.

Hydraulic Circuit of Drilling Machine

In a hydraulic drilling machine, the spindle rotation is driven by a hydraulic motor, and the feed (downward movement of the drill head) is controlled by a hydraulic cylinder with a flow control valve. Three operational phases: rapid approach → controlled feed (cutting) → rapid return.

Circuit Components:

  1. Tank: Hydraulic oil reservoir
  2. Pump: Fixed displacement gear pump driven by electric motor
  3. Relief Valve (RV): System pressure protection (typically 10–15 MPa)
  4. 4/3 Directional Control Valve (DCV): 4-port 3-position spring-centered valve; controls advance, dwell, and retract
  5. Flow Control Valve (FCV): Adjustable restrictor; controls drill feed rate
  6. Feed Cylinder: Double-acting cylinder; moves spindle head up/down
  7. Hydraulic Motor: Drives drill spindle rotation at required speed

Working Phases:

  • Rapid approach: DCV energised — oil flows freely through check valve (bypassing FCV) to cylinder top → drill head advances quickly to workpiece surface
  • Feed/Drilling: As drill contacts work, feed circuit activates — FCV restricts oil flow → cylinder advances at slow, controlled feed rate for proper cutting
  • Dwell: DCV in centre (blocked) position — cylinder stops; spindle motor continues for fine finishing
  • Retract: DCV reversed — oil to rod side of cylinder; drill head retracts rapidly; return oil from bore side flows back to tank

Foundry Shop — Cost Estimation Framework

A foundry casting cost estimate must account for all cost sections from pattern to finished casting:

  1. Pattern section (pattern making / amortization)
  2. Sand section (moulding sand preparation)
  3. Core section (core making if required)
  4. Mould section (mould preparation, pouring)
  5. Melting section (metal melting and pouring)
  6. Fettling section (cleaning, grinding, shot blasting)
Gross weight = Net weight × (1 + gate/riser allowance ratio)
Material cost = Gross weight × material rate (₹/kg)
Labour cost = Time per casting × labour rate (₹/hr)
Prime cost = Material cost + Labour cost
Overhead = Prime cost × overhead rate (%)
Factory cost = Prime cost + Overhead
Selling price = Factory cost × (1 + profit %)

Hydraulic Circuit of Shaper Machine

A shaper machine hydraulic circuit drives a double-acting cylinder that moves the ram (tool holder) back and forth. The fundamental requirement is slow cutting stroke + fast return stroke (quick return). This is achieved automatically by the difference in effective piston areas between bore side and rod side.

Circuit Components:

  1. Pump: Constant-pressure gear pump (motor-driven)
  2. Relief Valve (RV): Maximum system pressure protection; bypasses oil if cutting force causes overpressure
  3. 4/3 Directional Control Valve (DCV): 3-position valve; automatically reversed by trip dogs at end of each stroke
  4. Flow Control Valve (FCV): Adjustable restrictor on bore-side line; controls cutting speed
  5. Double-acting Cylinder: Bore side (full area A) for cutting stroke; rod side (area A−a) for return

Forward (Cutting) Stroke:

  1. DCV shifts to forward position
  2. Oil from pump → FCV → bore side of cylinder (full area side)
  3. FCV restricts flow → piston moves slowly at controlled cutting speed
  4. Return oil from rod side → DCV → tank
  5. Ram moves forward; tool cuts the workpiece

Return Stroke:

  1. Trip dog at end of cut reverses DCV automatically
  2. Oil from pump → rod side of cylinder (area = A − a)
  3. Oil bypasses FCV via check valve — unrestricted fast flow
  4. Return oil from bore side → DCV → tank
  5. Since rod-side area (A−a) < bore area (A), same flow Q gives higher velocity — quick return
Cutting speed v_c = Q / A_bore
Return speed v_r = Q / (A_bore − A_rod)
Since A_rod > 0 → v_r > v_c → Quick return automatically achieved
