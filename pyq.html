Apr 2024 · I(a)Explain briefly the term progression ratio.
3 marksCO1 · L2Module 1
View Answer

Progression ratio (φ) is the constant ratio between two adjacent spindle speeds when speeds are arranged in Geometric Progression.

φ = N₂/N₁ = N₃/N₂ = ... = (Nmax/Nmin)^(1/(n−1))

Ensures minimum speed loss and minimum number of gears. Permissible deviation ±10%.

Apr 2024 · I(c)Explain the principle of ultrasonic machining.
3 marksCO2 · L2Module 2
View Answer

Tool vibrates at 20–40 kHz, amplitude 25–100 μm. Abrasive slurry between tool and brittle workpiece. Vibrating tool hammers abrasive grains into work surface → brittle fracture → material removal. Static feed force advances tool. Transducer (PZT) converts electrical energy to mechanical vibration; horn amplifies amplitude.

→ Full explanation with diagram in Module 2

Apr 2024 · IIIExplain speed mechanism in lathe.
12 marksCO1 · L2Module 1
View Answer

Lathe speed mechanism: Motor → V-belt → Countershaft (constant speed) → Headstock gear box (multi-speed) → Spindle. Back gear arrangement gives extra low-speed range. All-geared headstock eliminates belt step-pulley.

Speed selection: Shift gear clusters in headstock. Norton gear box (tumbler gear) in feed mechanism selects thread pitch and feed rate. Key components: cone pulley (if belt drive), gear clusters, back gear, spindle nose.

→ See full explanation in Module 1

Apr 2024 · IVExplain Electro Chemical Machining with a neat diagram. Explain the process parameters in ECM.
12 marksCO2 · L2Module 2
View Answer

Principle: Reverse of electroplating. Workpiece is anode (+), tool is cathode (−). Electrolyte (NaCl/NaNO₃) flows in gap (0.05–1 mm). Anodic dissolution removes material from workpiece.

Diagram: Show DC supply, workpiece(+), tool(−), electrolyte flow in/out, servo-controlled feed, gap.

Process parameters: Voltage (5–30 V), current density (20–200 A/cm²), electrolyte type and concentration, feed rate, gap, temperature of electrolyte, electrode material.

Advantages: No tool wear, no HAZ, excellent surface finish (Ra 0.1–1.25 μm), high MRR for complex shapes.

→ See ECM section in Module 2

Sep 2024 · I(a)Briefly explain ray diagrams.
3 marksCO1 · L2Module 1
View Answer

Ray diagram is a graphical representation of the drive arrangement showing the specific values of all transmission ratios and speeds of all shafts. Vertical lines = shafts, horizontal lines = speed values on log scale, slanted lines (rays) = gear pairs. Slope of ray indicates transmission ratio.

→ See ray diagram section in Module 1

Sep 2024 · I(b)Describe stepped and stepless regulation of speeds.
3 marksCO1 · L1Module 1
View Answer

Stepped: Discrete speed steps via gear box. Fixed number of speeds. Low cost, high efficiency. Cannot get exact desired speed.

Stepless: Continuously variable speed (CVT, hydraulic, electric drives). Any speed in range achievable. Higher cost, used in CNC/precision machines.

→ See comparison table in Module 1

Sep 2024 · I(c)What are non-traditional machining processes? List and classify.
3 marksCO2 · L3Module 2
View Answer

NTM processes use alternative energy to remove material (not mechanical tool harder than work). Classification by energy source:

  • Mechanical: USM, AJM, WJM, AWJM
  • Thermal: EDM, EBM, LBM, PAM, IBM
  • Electrochemical: ECM
  • Chemical: Chemical milling, photochemical machining
Sep 2024 · I(d)Write short note on Plasma Arc Machining.
3 marksCO2 · L2Module 2
View Answer

PAM uses a high-velocity plasma jet at up to 28,000°C to melt and blow away material. Plasma formed by passing gas (Ar, N₂, H₂) through a constricted DC arc in a plasma torch. Used for cutting stainless steel, titanium, superalloys. HAZ is present; loud and UV radiation hazard.

→ See PAM section in Module 2

Sep 2024 · I(e)Describe the basic concept of powder metallurgy process.
3 marksCO3 · L2Module 3
View Answer

P/M is a process of making components from metallic powders. Process: Powder production → Mixing/Blending → Compacting (80–1600 MPa, green compact) → Sintering (below melting point, bonding) → Finishing. Produces near net shape parts with ~3% material waste.

Sep 2024 · I(g)Differentiate between cutaway and schematic circuit diagrams. Why are schematic diagrams preferred for hydraulic circuits?
3 marksCO4 · L2Module 4
View Answer

Cutaway: Physical cross-section of actual components; complex; difficult to trace fluid flow.

Schematic: Standardized JIC symbols; simple to read; clearly shows fluid flow paths; preferred for analysis and troubleshooting.

Schematic diagrams are preferred because any trained engineer can read them using JIC standards, and fluid flow paths are immediately clear.

Sep 2024 · I(h)Describe estimation and costing in a sheet metal shop.
3 marksCO4 · L2Module 4
View Answer

Sheet metal costing: Material cost (blank area × thickness × density × rate + scrap allowance), Labour (press cycles × rate/cycle), Direct expenses (die amortization per piece = die cost ÷ die life, power for press), Overheads (press depreciation, supervision as % of labour). Total cost = material + labour + direct + overheads.

Apr 2023 · Part AWhat is a speed chart? Explain its significance.
3 marksCO1Module 1
View Answer

A speed chart (ray diagram) is a graphical representation of the drive arrangement in a gear box. It shows all transmission ratios and spindle speeds on logarithmic scale. Significance: helps verify design, find exact transmission ratios, check if speed loss is acceptable.

Apr 2023 · Part ADefine step ratio. Differentiate between structural diagram and ray diagram.
3 marksCO1Module 1
View Answer

Step ratio: Constant ratio between adjacent spindle speeds in GP. φ = N(n+1)/Nₙ.

Structural diagram: Kinematic layout showing gear arrangement, shafts, and number of speed steps per stage. Does not show actual speed values.

Ray diagram: Graphical representation on log scale showing actual speed values at each shaft stage. Used to verify transmission ratios.

Apr 2023 · Part AExplain the working principle of Laser Beam Machining (LBM).
3 marksCO2Module 2
View Answer

Laser beam (coherent, monochromatic) focused to high energy density spot on workpiece. High energy density causes rapid heating → melting → vaporization. No vacuum required. Types: CO₂ (10.6 μm) for metals, Nd:YAG (1.06 μm) for precision drilling. No contact, no tool wear.

Apr 2023 · Part AExplain the function of a relief valve in a hydraulic system.
3 marksCO4Module 4
View Answer

Relief valve protects the pump and lines from excessive pressure. When system pressure exceeds the set value, the valve opens and returns fluid back to the reservoir. It sets the maximum system pressure and acts as a safety device. Symbol: rectangle with slant through it and spring.

Jul 2023 · Part AExplain the secondary operations in powder metallurgy.
3 marksCO3Module 3
View Answer

Three secondary operations: (1) Sizing — repressing in master die for dimensional accuracy; (2) Impregnation — filling pores with oil (self-lubricating bearings) or polymer resin (pressure-tight parts); (3) Infiltration — filling pores with molten metal (lower m.p.) by capillary action → nonporous, denser, stronger part.
